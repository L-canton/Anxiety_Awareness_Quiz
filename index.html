Anxiety Awareness Quiz

What are Anxiety Disorders?

We all feel worried or nervous sometimes. ​However, when regular feelings of anxiety begin to seriously impact our lives, including how we think, feel, and act that can be a sign of an anxiety disorder.

Do you think you know a lot about anxiety ​disorders? By taking this short quiz and sharing it around, you'll be helping bring awareness and destigmatize a very common problem that no one really talks about.

Anxiety disorders only affect sufferers mentally, not physically.

False

A common misconception about anxiety is that sufferers only experience mental (or cognitive) symptoms. In reality, anxiety can be so much more. Many physical symptoms such as a racing heart beat, shortness of breath, excessive sweating, panic attacks, etc. are experienced by people with anxiety every day. Source

How are anxiety disorders commonly treated?

All of these

Not all anxiety disorders are treated the same way. However, they're all treated with some combination of support groups, medications, and Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT). The idea is to supplement medication with stress management tools and coping strategies that help suffers better manage their symptoms. Source

Approximately how many Canadians are diagnosed with anxiety each year?

Over 4 million

That's more than 12% of the population. Anxiety and anxiety disorders are much more common than many people think and need to be addressed. Source

Is anxiety more likely to occur in men or women?

Women

In fact, women are twice as likely to experiece anxiety or an anxiety disorder than men. According to the Anxiety Disorders Association of Canada, 30% of women will experience an anxiety disorder during their lifetime. Source

People with anxiety are more likely to suffer from depression than those without anxiety.

True

It is very common for a person suffering from anxiety or an anxiety disorder to also suffer from depression. So common, that almost 50% of people diagnosed with Depression have also been diagnosed with an anxiety disorder at some point in their life. Source
